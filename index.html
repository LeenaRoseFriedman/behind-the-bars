The System Works Exactly As Designed

The United States has the highest incarceration rate in the world. Over 2 million people are currently imprisoned.

The system was built on the idea of rehabilitation—but in reality, it functions as punishment, control, and removal.

People are sent to prison for survival behaviors, untreated mental health issues, addiction, and poverty.

Children who’s parents struggle with addiction are twice as likely to go to jail

A recent study found that children of incarcerated parents are six times more likely than other children to develop a substance use disorder

“Nobody’s being corrected. It’s just the name.”

"Prison shouldn't be the first line of defense. It should be the last."

Why the Prison System Fails

  1. It punishes instead of treating
    Mental illness, trauma, and addiction are criminalized instead of addressed.
  2. It creates more harm
    Solitary confinement, violence, and isolation increase long-term instability.
  3. It blocks reentry
    People leave with records that prevent jobs, housing, and education.
  4. It targets specific communities
    Over-policing and systemic inequality funnel certain populations into prison.

— rather than rehabilitation

This is Not Random

It is not individual decisions that cause people to end up in prison.

People’s lives are shaped long before arrest through systemic inequality, instability, and survival environments.

The system steps in not to fix conditions, but to punish outcomes.

The Scale

Over 2 million people are incarcerated in the United States.

77% are rearrested within 5 years.

This is a cycle, not a solution.

A Voice From Inside

The system isn't meant for rehabilitation, putting a 12 year old boy who grew up in a bad situation, without the same opportunities as others, into solitary confinement, isn't protecting society, it's targetting minorities and people who are already vulnerable to systemic inequalities.

HangTime

HangTime is a program made to bring together ex-offenders, community leaders, and legal professionals into one family, united by similar adversities, and focuses on empowering those involved. The program is meant for those recovering from similar experiences, but anyone is welcome. In my time there, I have never felt any less a part of the community than anyone there. While being there, we learned about healthy habits for ones body, and it was so special sitting there learning all together, normalizing the importance of education for those who have never had the opportunity to learn in a safe and effective environment. In their words, “Our programs are designed to provide support and guidance to the youth in our communities. Through mentorship and community engagement, we aim to create a positive impact and foster personal growth.”
